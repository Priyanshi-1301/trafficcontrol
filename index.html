Intro

Many people in our neighborhood use the roadways to ensure their everyday necessities are met. This kind of task completion is always a joyful occasion when it happens swiftly. But just like us, a lot of individuals work continuously. They often restrict our path and occasionally might. The most typical example is the traffic signals on our roads.

These become really annoying when there is a long line of cars and you have an urgent task to perform. In essence, this is the outcome of poor traffic management, which needs to be rectified. The thing that irritates me the most is the RED signal at junctions. The color is most detested when it comes to traffic signals. Whether by choosing an alternative route, getting up earlier, or leaving our house sooner, we're always looking for ways to reduce the amount of time we spend stopping at red lights. Although there are ways to avoid this circumstance, there are times when it seems difficult to do so, in which case traffic management is crucial.

The main goal of this project is to shorten the amount of time that vehicles are stopped at red lights by using mathematical ideas as a guide.

Problem Statement

Evolve a method to minimize the red light time of all vehicular streams coming to a junction at a traffic signal by implicitly reducing the red light time using suitable mathematical theories.

The inclusion of volume of roads and size of the road in the problem may be neglected. It may be solved by keeping ideal conditions in mind.

Approach to the problem

1.Problem can be solved by a particular method which is applicable to all such sort of problems.

2. We look at the vehicles streams first, then make a compatibility graph out of it.

3. Then we have to divide the graph into cliques which would be calledas phases.

4. Each phase will be having a particular amount of time for green light.

5. The phase consists of certain streams.

6. We set some constraints like total time of 1 cycle and minimum amount of time of green light for each phase according to us.

7. Then giving variable names for phases, we follow the mathematics which gives us the least red light time of all streams.
